2026-06-19, by stable release v1.6314.0, published under tag stable-1.6314.0,
then published at https://github.com/EverestAPI/Everest. As of 2026-06-19, the

repository state. The term includes later official releases of that project.

The term also includes any project expressly and publicly designated as an
official successor by either:

(a) the persons who, immediately before the designation, were authorized under

(b) a publicly documented governance process established by a project already

A designation must identify the designated project and the date on which the
designation takes effect, and must be published in a reasonably permanent public
record. A designation does not remove any project already included in this
definition.

A fork, continuation, or other project does not become a Canonical Everest
Project merely by retaining the project's name, code, history, contributors, or
interfaces, or by claiming to be its successor.

"Everest" means only the code and other components that a Canonical Everest
Project publishes as, or expressly designates as, constituting or implementing
its official Mod-loading runtime or an interface intended for use by Mods or
Mod-development tools.

A file, dependency, tool, library, or other component does not become part of
Everest solely because it is stored in the Canonical Everest Project's
repository; included in the same package, release, distribution, or build
process; present in its dependency graph; or contained in or referenced by
another repository together with Everest, including as a submodule, subtree,
vendored copy, or dependency. Such a component is part of Everest only if it
independently satisfies the preceding definition.

"Mod" means a package, plugin, extension, add-on, or other similar work intended
to be loaded and run by Everest using its Mod-loading mechanisms.

"Third-Party Platform Component" means a third-party engine, middleware, SDK,
API, runtime library, tool, service, asset, asset format implementation,
importer, or related tooling that is expressly identified as required for game
functionality, Mod-loading functionality, or Mod-development functionality:

(a) by or on behalf of the authorized publisher or copyright holder of Celeste,
    in materials distributed with Celeste or in official public documentation;
    or
(b) by a Canonical Everest Project, in official release metadata or official
    public documentation.

Third-Party Platform Components do not include general-purpose operating system
components, system libraries, device drivers, hardware interfaces, generally
available implementations of language runtimes, or generally available
development tools.

"Target Platform" means:

1. Celeste and its assemblies, executable code, metadata, APIs, assets, and
   runtime components distributed by or on behalf of its authorized publisher as
   part of Celeste or expressly for official use with Celeste;
2. Everest;
3. any tool or framework, including MonoMod and Mono.Cecil, but only if and to
   the extent that the Canonical Everest Project expressly identifies that tool
   or framework in official release metadata or official public documentation as
   part of providing patching, detouring, reflection, instrumentation, assembly

4. SDK, import-library, stub, stripped, metadata-only, or interface-only forms
   of the components above; and
5. with respect to a particular Combined Work, Third-Party Platform Components,
   but only insofar as that Combined Work uses that component to Interoperate
   with one or more components described in items 1 through 4.

A portion of a work is not treated as a component of the Target Platform to the
extent that the portion itself constitutes part of the Library, the Linked
Version, or another covered work based on the Library. Combining, packaging, or
distributing such a portion with a Target Platform component does not cause that
portion to become part of the Target Platform.

"Interoperate" means, with respect to two or more components, to communicate
with, call into, be called by, reference, inspect, patch, hook, subscribe to
events from, replace or alter behavior in, extend, load into, be loaded by, or
otherwise use interfaces, metadata, code, runtime behavior, events, entry
points, symbols, types, members, assets, files, or services of one component
from another component.

"Platform Constraint" means a restriction, omission, inability, obstruction, or
technical failure relevant to a permission granted by this exception.

A Platform Constraint is "attributable solely to the Target Platform" only to
the extent that:

(a) it results from technical behavior inherent in one or more components of the
    Target Platform, or from terms that, in the relevant respect, govern access
    to or use of only one or more components of the Target Platform;
(b) in the absence of that behavior or those terms, the Platform Constraint
    would not arise in substantially the same form or to substantially the same
    extent; and
(c) neither the Library, the Linked Version, nor any non-Target-Platform portion
    of the Application or Combined Work independently imposes the same
    restriction or would independently cause substantially the same omission,
    inability, obstruction, or failure.

No additional permission applies to the extent that you, a person acting at your
direction, or a person knowingly cooperating with you for that purpose caused or
substantially increased a Platform Constraint for the purpose of avoiding a
requirement of LGPLv3 section 4.

Grant and Scope of Additional Permission:

When you convey a Combined Work that is itself a Mod for Celeste, or a Combined
Work that Interoperates with the Target Platform for the purpose of developing,
compiling, installing, loading, running, testing, or distributing Mods for
Celeste, the following additional permissions apply. Except as expressly
provided below, LGPLv3 section 4 continues to apply.

For purposes of the introductory paragraph of LGPLv3 section 4, a restriction
arising from terms independently governing only a Target Platform component
shall be disregarded to the extent that its relevant effect is a Platform
Constraint attributable solely to the Target Platform.

No term imposed by you, or governing the Library, the Linked Version, or a
non-Target-Platform portion of the Application or Combined Work, is disregarded
under the preceding paragraph.

The preceding paragraphs affect only the introductory paragraph of LGPLv3
section 4. They do not excuse any failure to satisfy sections 4(a) through 4(e),
except as expressly provided below.

For purposes of LGPLv3 section 4(c), an execution-time display or collection of
copyright notices shall be disregarded in determining whether the Combined Work
displays copyright notices during execution if the contents and presentation of
that display or collection are controlled solely by one or more Target Platform
components.

The preceding permission does not apply if a non-Target-Platform portion of the
Application or Combined Work can add the Library's copyright notice and the
required license reference to that display or collection through an interface,
extension point, or configuration facility that the Target Platform ordinarily
provides for adding notices or other content to that display or collection.

The mere technical possibility of producing the same or a similar result does
not constitute such an ability. In particular, no such ability arises solely
from the possibility of modifying or replacing a Target Platform component;
altering its executable code, memory, data, assets, files, or stored resources;
using a general-purpose patching, hooking, interception, rewriting, rendering,
or overlay mechanism; exercising elevated operating-system, kernel, device, or
hardware privileges; circumventing a technical measure; or superimposing a
separate display.

The preceding paragraph applies even if the Target Platform ordinarily makes
the general-purpose mechanism available for use by Mods or Mod-development
tools, unless that mechanism includes a facility ordinarily intended for adding
content to the relevant display or collection.

The additional permission concerning LGPLv3 section 4(c) applies only if each
conveyed copy of the Combined Work is accompanied by a readily accessible
notice containing the Library's copyright notice and a reference directing the
recipient to the accompanying copies of GPLv3 and LGPLv3.
